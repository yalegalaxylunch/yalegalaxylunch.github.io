Galaxy Lunch is a Yale Astronomy Department seminar focused on discussing recent advancements in the subfield of galaxy formation and evolution. This seminar provides a space for discussion about a wide range of topics, from the earliest galaxies in the Universe to supermassive black holes within galaxies to the properties of our own Galaxy. The seminar meets once a week on Wednesdays during the academic year; the first 30-40 minutes of each session is reserved for a long-format presentation, followed by discussion and questions from attendees. Members of the department are invited to join in listening to the talks -- typically given by external speakers -- and participate in discussions following the talk, providing a way to stay up to date with developments in relevant topics and for members of Yale Astronomy to interact with new advancements in galactic astronomy coming out of other institutions.

This seminar has been an established organization in the Astronomy Department for over a decade; this year the organizers are Chloe Neufeld (3rd year graduate student) and Sebastian Monzon (4th year graduate student). The organizers of the seminar solicit interest in giving long-form presentations at the beginning of every term; the seminar is well-known throughout external institutions and slots tend to fill up rapidly. 

Our seminar is open to all members of the department, from faculty to undergraduates, and we aim to be a welcoming place for all our attendees. We encourage everyone to attend regardless of familiarity with session topics, and we ask all of our speakers to ensure their presentations are accessible to all audience members in any astrophysics field.
